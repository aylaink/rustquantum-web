Pipeline

From molecule definition to quantum hardware execution, grouped in five phases.

Phase 1 — Problem definition

Molecule and basis set. You choose the molecule and the single-particle space in which you represent it. This is pure input; everything downstream depends on these two choices.

Molecule

Atomic coordinates, nuclear charges, overall charge, and spin multiplicity.

Basis set

Gaussian-type orbitals (STO-3G, 6-31G, def2-SVP, …) expanding the one-particle Hilbert space.

Phase 2 — Classical preprocessing

Integrals, SCF, and Hamiltonian. The trio that produces the molecular Hamiltonian in second quantization. Still classical computational chemistry; the QPU has not entered the scene yet.

Integrals

One-electron overlap, kinetic, and nuclear-attraction integrals plus the two-electron repulsion tensor (ERI) over the basis.

SCF

Hartree–Fock self-consistent field solution for the mean-field reference determinant.

Hamiltonian

Second-quantized molecular Hamiltonian with one- and two-body coefficients hpq and hpqrs expressed in the MO basis.

Phase 3 — Quantization

Fermion mapping and compression. Here you cross the classical→quantum frontier: you map fermionic operators to qubits and apply symmetry reductions (tapering, frozen core, parity) before building the circuit. Compression belongs here, not at the end, because shrinking the Pauli operator before the ansatz is far cheaper than recompiling afterwards.

Fermion mapping

Jordan–Wigner or Bravyi–Kitaev mapping turns fermionic ladder operators into strings of Pauli operators on qubits.

Compression

Z2 symmetry tapering, frozen-core reduction, and parity-preserving consolidations remove redundant qubits and Pauli terms.

Phase 4 — Variational construction and optimization

Circuit ansatz and VQE loop. The parameterized circuit and the classical–quantum optimization loop. It is an inner sub-loop: the classical optimizer calls the quantum circuit many times.

Circuit ansatz

Parameterized quantum circuit (UCCSD, hardware-efficient, ADAPT-VQE) whose expectation value approximates the ground state.

VQE loop

Classical optimizer (COBYLA, L-BFGS-B, SPSA) iterates on the circuit parameters using the measured expectation value of the Hamiltonian as the cost function.

Phase 5 — Execution

Transpilation and hardware. Take the abstract circuit down to the physical backend and run it with error mitigation.

Transpilation

Rewrite the circuit into the target backend's native gate set and couple it to its qubit connectivity graph (heavy-hex, all-to-all, …).

Hardware

Execution on a QPU (IBM Q, IonQ, Quantinuum, …) with shot sampling and error mitigation (zero-noise extrapolation, readout correction, dynamical decoupling).
