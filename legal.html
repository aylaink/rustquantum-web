Privacy

Who we are

This website is operated by Ignacio López Rodríguez (data controller under the GDPR), as a personal learning project, from Spain under the domain rustquantum.com. For any privacy-related request, email info@rustquantum.com or use the contact form.

What data we collect

Contact form

When you submit the contact form, we collect:

  • Your name
  • Your email address
  • The subject and message you write

This data is sent via Cloudflare Workers to the Resend email API, which delivers it to our inbox. We do not store your data on any server or database beyond what is necessary to receive and respond to your message.

Analytics

We use Cloudflare Web Analytics, a cookieless and privacy-preserving analytics service. It does not set cookies, does not fingerprint visitors, and does not persist any identifier on your device. It collects only aggregated page-view counts, referrer, country, and device category. No personal data is processed, so no consent banner is shown.

Cookies

Summary

This site does not use cookies for analytics, advertising, or profiling, and does not write to localStorage or sessionStorage. The only cookie set on your browser is a strictly-necessary security cookie placed by Cloudflare; no consent is required for it under Art. 22 LSSI-CE / the ePrivacy Directive.

Strictly necessary cookies

These are required for the website to function and cannot be disabled. Cloudflare sets __cf_bm automatically to protect against malicious traffic.

Analytics (no cookies)

We use Cloudflare Web Analytics, a cookieless, privacy-preserving service. It does not set cookies, does not fingerprint visitors, and does not persist any identifier on your device. It collects only aggregated page-view counts, referrer, country, and device category. No personal data is processed and no cross-site tracking takes place. Because no information is stored on or read from your device, Art. 22 consent does not apply.

How to manage cookies

You can control cookies through your browser settings:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy

Disabling cookies may affect website functionality.

Data redistribution attribution

Basis Set Exchange (CC-BY-4.0)

The numerical basis-set values shown in the Basis stage validation tables (STO-3G, STO-6G, 6-31G, 6-31G(d), 6-311G, def2-SVP exponents and contraction coefficients) originate from the Basis Set Exchange and are therefore a redistribution of CC-BY-4.0 data. Attribution is given via the "Basis Set Exchange (BSE)" entry in Sources, linking to basissetexchange.org, and via the primary publication citations in the "Basis sets" section of the same page.

The BSE web application itself (the Python code) is BSD-3-Clause; only the data values are CC-BY-4.0.

Public-domain data

Numerical values from CODATA 2022 (constants), IUPAC / CIAAW 2024 (atomic masses), and NIST CCCBDB (molecular geometries) are published by government-funded bodies and are in the public domain or freely reproducible with attribution. Attribution is given through Sources; no license text applies.
